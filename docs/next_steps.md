### Component 8: Documentation

#### [NEW] [income_vs_spend_clarifications.md](file:///c:/work/personal/finance/bank-ledger-dashboard/docs/income_vs_spend_clarifications.md)
Comprehensive documentation covering:
- **Why expense > income is possible**:
  1. **Credit card spending**: CC payments show as debits from bank, but the original spending wasn't captured (no CC statement imported). So the CC bill payment is "expense" but the actual income that funded it happened earlier via salary.
  2. **Cash withdrawals**: ATM withdrawals are debits (expense) but subsequent cash spending isn't tracked. Cash received isn't captured either.
  3. **Inter-bank transfers not fully detected**: If some transfers between own accounts aren't caught by self-transfer rules, they inflate both income and expense.
  4. **Investment outflows**: If some investment transfers are miscategorized as expenses.
  5. **Loan disbursements**: Loan credits are counted as income, loan EMI repayments as expense — but the principal came from outside the tracked accounts.
  6. **Missing bank statements**: If a receiving bank's statement isn't imported, money sent there is "expense" with no corresponding "income" on the other side.
- **What "Net (real)" means**: `Real Income − Real Expense` where "Real" means self-transfers and duplicates are excluded. It represents the net money flow from the perspective of external interactions only.
- **Corrected headline breakdown**:
  - Raw credits: ~₹1.01 Cr  |  Raw debits: ~₹1.01 Cr
